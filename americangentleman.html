The Gentleman's Bookshelf: This Side of Paradise by F. Scott Fitzgerald

In 1920, at the age of 24, F. Scott Fitzgerald published this semi-autobiographical novel about Amory Blaine, a young attractive Princeton gentleman growing up in elite  Post World War I America. Broken up into 3 books, the novel is a combination of Fitzgerald’s first manuscript “The Romantic Egoist” along with two other significant periods in his life, “The Interlude” and  ”The Education of a Personage.” The book chronicles Amory’s schooling at St. Regis Preparatory School, then Princeton, Amory joining the army in World War I, and his love and loss of two débutantes. It concludes with Amory’s iconic lament, ” I know myself, that is all.”

Taking its title from a line of the Rupert Brooke poem Tiare Tahiti, This Side of Paradise is an elegant crafting of prose and poetry which illustrates the lavish lifestyle of Upper-class America the early 1900s. 

This novel reads like a lesson in high society, as well as serves as a guide book for the young man in America. It should be a staple of every Gentleman’s book shelf.  Written in a style that combines narrative, verse and letters to and from Amory, the novel is unlike Fitzgerald’s contemporaries. Heralded by the Chicago Tribune as, “the only adequate study that we have had of the contemporary American in adolescence and young manhood,” This Side of Paradise is a study of the American Gentleman for the American Gentleman.

Basics: A Note on Grooming

It is the duty of every gentleman to ensure that he is well kept and presents himself appropriately. Although it is sure to be your charm, wit, and pleasant demeanor that wins over that young woman or seals the deal in that business presentation, the way you look can affect the way you feel and ultimately how others feel about you before you even open your mouth. Here are some tips on the fundamentals that will send you well on your way to looking and feeling great – grooming.

Fingernails

Whether you are shaking a client’s hand, holding a door for that lovely gal, or gracefully wielding a cocktail at dinner, one of the most looked upon aspects of your body is your hands. While they may previously have come as an afterthought, the first thing someone notices when you are motioning with your hand or gripping a glass is your fingernails. It is important to keep your nails looking good because it is a perfect indicator to someone of the quality of your hygiene and your attention to detail.

To keep your nails looking prim, it is good practice to trim them about every 5-7 days. You most definitely do not want your nails peeking over the tips or your fingers. However, when you trim them, you also do not want to cut them all the way down to the cuticles because it is unsightly and quite frankly, can be painful. When trimming your nails, you should not just chop them off at the ends. They should be rounded at the corners so as to create a nice ovular shape. A nail file is useful in obtaining good shape to your nails.

The above information can be repeated about one’s toe nails and comes in handy in the warm months when wearing sandals or enjoying a drink poolside.

Haircut

The most alterable aspect of a gentleman’s natural physique, the haircut can take many forms. While it may be tempting to do so, don’t let your haircut define you. Though it is on the top of your head for everyone to see, it should not be the focus. People should be focused on your eyes and your mouth. The former is where you want them to look and the latter is what you want them to listen to and that is what should be of primary concern to you. That being said, it is still important to make sure that your haircut is classy, but conservative.

I am not here to tell you how to style your hair, but there are some basic guidelines that should be followed when addressing your hair. The volume of your hair-do should never be that much greater than the volume of your skull. There should never be a question as to whether a meadowlark is going to fly out of a mop on top of your head. Also, spikes of any kind are expressly prohibited. Your hair is not naturally spiky and there is no reason why you should want to make it that way. Outside of rock bands, hair should never be long enough to hang off of your head. While everyone’s hair grows at different rates, it is usually good practice to get a hair cut every 1-1 ½ months. When your hair starts to naturally lay over your ears, you know you are past due for a cut.

You should trust your hair cut to salon or barbershop where you have a relationship with the professional. Getting a haircut is more than the person just cutting your hair. It is important to build a relationship with him or her. This is important so they know what you want when you come in, how your hair naturally lays, the correct way to texture your hair, and so they can be friendly with you. Every person’s hair is different and it is important that person becomes familiar with it. You should have a good enough relationship with your barber or stylist to invite them to your wedding, or at least a nice dinner.

As far as styling your hair, you can choose to put product in it or just go all natural. Whatever you do, your hair should not be goopy, stiff or shiny. Your hair should not still be perfect after a windy day. Good product to go with is one that will provide a matte finish and still remain pliable throughout the day. Body Hair

Unless your gal expressly tells you that she enjoys a nice mane, it is good practice to keep your body hair to a minimum. Now, different gentlemen can have differing degrees of body hair, so it is up to you to choose your method of trimming (scissors, razor, tweezers, etc). Just know that it should never be so thick to where you can’t see the skin beneath the hair. Also, don’t let it accumulate at the nipples or around the naval.

Every gentleman should have a handheld mirror as well so they can inspect the back of their neck and their back. It is good practice to trim your neck hair every few days so as to keep your hairline neat. A razor is generally a sufficient device for this.

Facial Hair

Much like body hair, unless you have been told by your lady to keep a bear/goatee/mustache, you should not have one. Some women like hair on their men’s faces and that is up to them, but generally, a gentleman is clean cut. However, it is okay to let a couple days of stubble grow as long as you do not have an event and you are not working (read: it’s the weekend). This is also a very good indicator to someone about how much you care about your appearance, and a gentleman should always be concerned with his appearance.

Those are the basics of what every gentleman should know about grooming. If you continue to brush your teeth, keep those eyebrows, nose hairs, and ear hairs trimmed and follow the simple rules stated above, you will be well on your way to feeling good about yourself and consequently having others feel good about you. Arnold Palmer: American Gentleman

September 10th was The King’s birthday (I know we’re a little late to the party), but my Father shared this story of Arnold Palmer with me that every Gentleman should read.

Greatness Comes in All Forms

Before the 1993 Senior British Open, which was scheduled to be contested over the links of Royal Lytham & St. Annes, Arnold Palmer received a letter from a Mr. Hans Bolton of Lancashire , England . The letter containing, as it did, the language of someone who knew nothing about the game, might have offended a superstar too full of his own importance. In Palmer’s case, the handwritten letter caught his attention.


The letter, which came into the possession of a friend several years later, contains this remark in the opening paragraph, “I must admit I am not a golf fan. I realize that you must be extremely busy, being one of the “big names” in the golfing fraternity.” Bolton then talks about the importance of the upcoming Senior Open but adds, “An even more important event is taking place (at least to me anyway). You see, I am getting married to my fiancée Sally Anne Murphy, a truly gorgeous and wonderful girl.” The problem, as Bolton unfolds, is simple, neither of the honeymoon suites at the Clifton Arms Hotel is available because, as it was explained to him, “Mr. Arnold Palmer is staying in one and Mr. Gary Player is staying in the other, and they have been booked for ages!”


The letter then proceeded to paint an emotional portrait. “My fiancée was, of course, distraught at this news, convinced that our big day is doomed to failure. Something I suppose, like a triple bogey on the 1st hole.” Then Bolton makes a daring suggestion. “I was just wondering if perhaps, there would be any chance at all of you swapping rooms with us, just for one night. This is a shot in the dark, I really don’t expect that you will, but one can only try. You see I work for the Sunblest Bread Company and Sally works as a children’s nanny so this is probably the only chance we will ever have of staying anywhere as grand as the Clifton Arms.”
He signed the letter “Yours hopefully,” and then added two postscripts. P.S. You are more than welcome to join us for a drink at the reception. P.P.S. I didn’t write to Gary Player because we were told you were a much nicer chap!
Palmer, of course, not only surrendered the suite but took them up on the offer of the beer where he drank a toast to the happy couple and even posed for a photograph. 

No matter who you are, remember,  the smallest acts of civility and kindness can go the longest way in making someone’s day. Be a true American Gentleman.

Gentleman's Essentials: The Navy Blazer

Okay, I’ll admit it. I’ve gotten a little blazer happy. But it’s fall and I’m tired of seeing so many Affliction Hoodies, that something had to be done. A Gentleman should never commit that faux pas. Despite this “trend”, there is no denying that the blue (read navy) blazer is a cornerstone of a Gentleman’s wardrobe. The Preppy Handbook goes so far as to note that “every single one of us owns a Blue Blazer” and you should be no exception. A classic two button blue blazer, complete with gold buttons will make any Gentleman feel like he can take on the world (or at least conquer happy hour).

Why You Should Own One:

The blue blazer is classic American style. Your Grandfather wore one, your Father wore one, now it’s high time that you carried on the Gentlemanly tradition. It pairs with jeans or khakis, can be worn in any social situation that requires only a jacket. It never seems to look too dressed up, giving you a casual feel with a little sharper look. The blazer gives you the option of dressing up your every day look while also allowing you the luxury of giving her your jacket when she’s cold. 

Where You Can Get It:

This staple is not hard to come by, and depending on the quality of the blazer they can range from a few dollars to a month’s rent. Brooks Brother’s offers The Classic 2-Button Blazer  for $528 dollars. While this is the quintessential jacket, it’s a little too rich for my blood (no pun intended). If you’re not willing to spend the big bucks, it’s time to hunt one of these blazer’s down.

  • First start with your father or grandfather. There is a high likelihood they have an extra jacket that you could borrow (read keep). Make sure to have it tailored to fit.
  • Never underestimate the power of the internet. Scour ebay, Gilt.com, even j.crew and Ralph Lauren sales will sometimes have a treasure trove of jackets
  • Be sure to check your local vintage store. If you hunt long enough you’re bound to find something you like. Make sure to bring it to your tailor.

These jackets were so common that it’s not difficult to find one at a steal, if you’re willing to hunt long and hard enough (I found my Brooks Brothers blazer for $6 at a vintage store). 

How You Should Wear It:

Dress up the blazer with a nice tie and a button down shirt or ditch the tie for a more casual look (just be sure to remember Lesson No. 10). Also never pair the navy blazer with black trousers and a black belt, you might as well be wearing a graphic jacket with the words “Ed Hardy” embroidered on it at that point. The most important thing to remember is to wear the jacket however you’re comfortable, and always with a classy elegance Don’t forget to get it tailored!

Happy Thanksgiving - here's a tip

The American Gentleman plays an active role in all aspects of Thanksgiving Dinner.  Even if you aren’t the iron chef, there are plenty of ways to help on Thanksgiving day. Want to impress your family (or her family) learn how to set the table properly. 

Here’s how to set the table.

  1. How many people will be in attendance?  If there are children coming, consider a separate children’s table.
  2. Where do you want everyone to sit?  Consider a few matters of convenience such as the cook sitting near the kitchen door, parents sitting next to children, or which people would interact best with others.  There are also matters of tradition to consider, such as a male guest traditionally being seated on the hostess’s Left, and a female guest traditionally being seated at the host’s right.  Often times people alternate between men and women, but this is no longer necessary.
  3. Do I use a tablecloth?  It’s entirely up to the host, and depends what type of food you are serving and mood you hope to create.  If you choose to use a table cloth, remember to have the middle crease run straight down the center of the table and it should hang about 18 inches off the table.  If it’s a buffet dinner, it should hang to the floor.
  4. Where do I position the plates, glasses and utensils?  Use the picture below to guide you:

A few things to remember:

  • Fold the napkins and place them in either the center of each diner’s place, under the forks on the left, in an empty water glass.
  • Place the large dinner fork to the left and the smaller salad for to the left of the dinner fork.
  • Place a knife to the right of the napkin, with the cutting edge toward the plate.  This is an old custom to prevent aggressive behavior at the dinner table.    

When do I begin clearing the table?  When the last person if finished eating, you may begin to clear the plates.  When the diner has finished, he/she signals this by setting the fork and knife parallel to each other, so they lie either horizontally across the plate or on the diagnal at approximately 4 o’clock.

Setting the table seems like a small gesture, but if done well can be very impressive. Clearing the table is also a great way to show that you’re thoughtful and considerate without having to do a whole lot of actual work.  Now that you’ve done your part, grab a seat on the couch and enjoy some football and a nap!

Happy Thanksgiving from the American Gentleman!

Belle of the Ball Presents: 5 Ways to Impress a Lady on a First Date

Belle of the Ball is a new featured column we will be running in coordination with Chelsea at Southern Gent Gifts, our resident Belle of the Ball. Be sure to check them out for great gift ideas for yourself or someone special.

  So often I hear men say that they’ve been on hundreds of first dates, but very few second dates. It’s no mystery that a first date can be a nerve wracking experience. It is also very easy to screw up. But fear not Gentlemen, with these five easy steps you will be on your way to a second date in no time. 



First and foremost CALL AND HAVE A PLAN   
 

I understand that it is so much easier to text “let’s hangout Wednesday” BUT that gives a girl no direction. When you make the decision that you would like to spend time with us here are a few KEY things that should be remembered.

  • CALL- pick up the phone and call the lady you are interested in seeing, this is a small gesture that we all appreciate. A call can be nerve wracking for some, but if you don’t have the guts to call her, how do you plan to sit through an entire date’s worth of conversation? Always call!
  • DATE AND TIME- Let the girl know a date and time of the actual date. This is key for many aspects from attire to scheduling. She needs to know if you’re going out at night, if it’s a casual dinner or if you’re going to the symphony. Planning is important, a first date is not the best time for spontaneity. 
  • RESERVATIONS - If possible make reservations. Do not show up with no plan in mind. We want to spend time with you, so make life easier by planning ahead with reservations. A woman is impressed when you can walk in, simply say your name, and be led to a table for two. 


Second, DON’T FORGET SIMPLE GESTURES.

Pick the lady up at her place and come to the door. Do not wait in your car. If we are running late don’t worry, remember, we are trying to impress you and have taken the time to do so. Three simple gestures that you can make that will go a long way are:

  • Open the car door.  
  • Open the door at the restaurant. 
  • Pull out her chair. 

    These simple things may not seem like much, but growing up most of us have seen our dads perform these simple gestures daily with our mothers. Old school, classic, and shows respect.

    Third, KEEP THE CONVERSATION ON A POSITIVE NOTE.

    You may be having tons of problems. You may HATE your job,may be failing a class, in a fight with your parents, arguing with your roommate, you feel broke, your ex is evil, or all of the above. And yes we are willing to listen, but we agreed to a DATE. We agreed to spend time with you because we want to get to know you. We do not want to be your psychiatrist, especially when trying to have a good time. If you can’t think of anything to say, or are having an off day, or even really want to impress us, ask questions! The easiest way to strike up a conversation with anyone, especially a woman on the first date is to ask her questions. But be careful to keep the questions grounded a bit, don’t ask her if she plans to have kids or when she wants to get married. Save those for date 197, not date number 1. 


    Fourth, CASUALLY PAY WITHOUT US NOTICING.

    There is nothing more awkward than the check sitting on the table between us staring us in the face. Even if you are going to pay and planning on it, at that moment we are debating whether we should offer or if we even brought cash or cards. Before the check arrives, slip off to the bathroom or to the bar for after dinner drinks, grab the waiter or a hostess, slip her your credit card and pay the bill. That way when the check comes back, it is already taken care of and awkward moment avoided.

    Fifth and FINALLY, FINISH THE DATE.

    Even if you plan on going out after, make sure to close the date. Take us home, tell us you had a great time. It’s always difficult to know what to do after dinner, whether you take her out with your friends or go grab after dinner drinks. Even so, make sure that we get home okay. MOST importantly - call the next day and tell us thank you. If you don’t see a future, a simple text is fine. Just remember to end on a good note because even if nothing happens in the future, it is better to be known as a Gentleman than a jerk. Girls talk, it will get around that you were not courteous and respectful.

    Follow these five simple rules and you will be will on your way to a second date. What you do from there is up to you. Remember always be a gentleman and keep it classy.  - cHc
